Transfer boundary

The service helps devices meet. It does not keep your file.

8ma Quick Transfer uses a WebRTC DataChannel to move file data between browsers. The service handles signaling and temporary rooms, not persistent file storage.

The precise version: if the devices cannot establish a direct connection, the browser may relay encrypted traffic through TURN. Relaying improves connection success without creating a long-lived downloadable copy.

Questions

What you may want to know first.

Do I need to install an app?
No. Senders and receivers can open 8ma Quick Transfer directly in a modern browser. A current version of Chrome or Edge is recommended.
Are files stored on a server?
File contents are not stored persistently. The service helps devices connect. Encrypted WebRTC traffic may be relayed through TURN on restrictive networks, but this does not create a long-lived file copy.
How do I send a file to another device?
Choose a file, then share the generated link, QR code, or 8-character pickup code. Keep both pages open until the receiver completes the download.
Can more than one person receive a file?
Yes. One share supports up to three concurrent receivers, and the sending page shows each device's connection and transfer progress.
Why can a connection fail?
Peer-to-peer connections can be affected by browser versions, company firewalls, carrier networks, and device sleep policies. Keep the page in the foreground, update the browser, or try another network.
