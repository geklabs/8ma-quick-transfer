Questions

What you may want to know first.

Do I need to install an app?
No. Senders and receivers can open 8ma Quick Transfer directly in a modern browser. A current version of Chrome or Edge is recommended.
Does 8ma Quick Transfer keep a copy of the file?
No. 8ma Quick Transfer does not keep a long-lived downloadable copy. Keep both pages open during transfer; the share ends when the session is over.
How do I send a file to another device?
On the same WiFi, open the site on both devices and enable Nearby Sharing on the sender. The receiving device can then see the file. On different networks, use a link, QR code, or 8-character pickup code.
Can more than one person receive a file?
Yes. One share supports up to three concurrent receivers, and the sending page shows each device's connection and transfer progress.
Why can a connection fail?
Device state, browser version, and network conditions can affect a transfer. Keep the page in the foreground, use the current Chrome or Edge, or try another network.
Do I need to start over after an interruption?
Not always. Supported browsers keep the completed portion on the receiving device. Once the sender confirms the original file again, the receiver can continue from a verified checkpoint.
